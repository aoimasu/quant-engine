# QE-466 — Vintage leaderboard / comparison surface (informational, NOT a selector)  *(R1 — inspection)*

> **Status:** Todo — [design QE-455](../../architecture/qe-455-research-flow-design.md#9-the-leaderboard--comparison-surface) · [backlog index](../../backlog.md#research-flow) · [spec](../../specs.md)

`Phase: Research-flow (R1)` · `Area: backend / frontend` · `Depends on: QE-467, QE-460, QE-456, QE-457, QE-430`

**Why.** A research operator wants to *see* which sealed vintage produced the best tradable strategy and how
steer/params differed across runs. But this is exactly where the design's dominant risk lives: a leaderboard
that becomes an **outer best-of-N selector** re-introduces the uncounted multiple-testing QE-430..454 killed
(design §1/§3/§11.1). This ticket builds the comparison surface **as inspection only**, structurally incapable
of selecting/promoting.

**Scope / requirements.**
- A read-only leaderboard/comparison over **already-sealed** vintages (consuming QE-456 detail + the QE-457
  rendering), ranking on the **persisted, tradable, deflation-honest** metrics **only** (all read from QE-467's
  sealed evidence — never recomputed):
  - **net-of-cost** performance from the **persisted net-of-cost holdout return series on the DEPLOYED
    capacity-capped weights** (QE-467/438) — never gross Sharpe, equal-weight, or a lone Sharpe (the QE-450
    §13.5 inversion),
  - **capacity-at-size** (`capacity_usd`, QE-467/QE-128/433) and **realised turnover** (QE-467),
  - **cross-vintage correlation** computed over the persisted net-of-cost series with **QE-430 R(N) / Fisher-z
    sample-size deflation**, **surfacing the effective N** — are these vintages diverse or the same bet re-drawn?
- Show **steer/param diffs** (indicator subset, budget, windows — from the QE-467 steer delta) per vintage so the
  operator understands the frontier.
- **ENFORCE (not merely display) the holdout-consultation budget.** Surface each vintage's overlap-keyed
  holdout-consultation exposure (QE-460/467) AND act on it: **grey-out / escalate the DSR bar** on vintages whose
  holdout has been **over-consulted**, so the top slot cannot be "improved" by re-runs. Choose ONE enforcement
  posture and state it: EITHER apply a **max-statistic / SPA correction across the displayed set**, OR **rank
  only on each vintage's own already-deflated evidence with NO fresh cross-vintage selection statistic on holdout
  verdicts**. The leaderboard must never become a fresh selection statistic over holdout verdicts.
- **Structurally not a selector:** expose **no** promote / select-best / auto-run action or endpoint; it is a
  view over sealed artefacts. Promotion to a runtime vintage stays through the *existing per-run G1 gate +
  seal* — ranking confers no additional blessing. Every vintage is labelled **"backtest-holdout only — not
  paper-confirmed"** (promotion still owes the G2/G3 live/shadow gates).

**Out of scope.** Any "promote the top vintage" / "select best" action (**explicitly rejected**, §3/§9). Any
automated re-run / best-of-N loop fed by the ranking. Gross-Sharpe or in-sample ranking. Recomputing the
net-of-cost series (it is read from QE-467). Changing the gate/seal. The detail endpoint (QE-456) and inspector
(QE-457) it reuses.

**Acceptance criteria.**
- The leaderboard ranks sealed vintages on the **persisted** net-of-cost holdout series (deployed capacity-capped
  weights) / capacity-at-size / turnover / cross-vintage correlation (QE-430 R(N)/Fisher-z, effective N shown)
  and shows steer/param diffs; gross Sharpe, equal-weight, lone-Sharpe, and in-sample metrics are absent.
- The consultation budget is **enforced, not just shown**: over-consulted vintages have their DSR bar
  greyed-out/escalated; the surface either applies a max-statistic/SPA correction across the displayed set or
  ranks only on each vintage's own already-deflated evidence with no fresh cross-vintage selection statistic —
  the chosen posture is stated and tested.
- It adds **no** endpoint or UI action that seals, promotes, selects, or auto-re-runs — asserted by a test that
  the surface is read-only over sealed vintages; every vintage is labelled "not paper-confirmed"; promotion
  remains via the existing gate/seal (and still owes G2/G3).
- A standing caveat states cross-vintage ranking is inspection, and that acting on it by re-running until the
  top slot improves is the rejected best-of-N pattern.
