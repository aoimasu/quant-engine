## Decision summary

Lives in `qe-risk` (shared: WFO calibration + runtime QE-212). `MarkEma` is the τ½=60s smoothed-mark
observer (`alpha = 1 − 2^(−tick/half_life)`, `Decimal` — no float money; raw-mark fast-tier A3 documented,
D1). The three-tier `CircuitBreaker` fires **Fast** (rapid drop over `fast_window`, speed) > **Med** >
**Slow** (depth), a pure function of the equity stream so `replay()` runs it on history (D2). The
per-vintage `CalibrationProfile` sidecar (per-strategy / per-cohort slow+fast DD / ensemble fast-drop,
serde) is calibrated from the **observed** drawdown distribution (`calibrate_threshold` = quantile·margin,
spec A2 baseline, D3/D4); distribution-agnostic so the OOS/stressed alternative is parameter-only.

## Review (pass 1) — Approved

Principal-level review of `a3176d7`. Both ACs met; EMA and breaker math correct; replay fires all three
tiers via distinct mechanisms. No real bugs.

- **(a)** D4 baseline = A2 observed-behaviour sidecar; OOS/stressed (D4) and A3 raw-mark fast-tier (D1)
  alternatives documented with rationale, not baseline.
- **(b)** `alpha = 1 − 0.5^(tick/half_life)`, seed-on-first-sample, non-positive half-life ⇒ no smoothing;
  half-life property exact by construction (100→200 step reaches 150 after 60 ticks).
- **(c)** All-time peak drives total drawdown; rolling `fast_window+1` window drives the speed measure;
  Fast > Med > Slow with `slow_dd < med_dd`; `fast_thresh > 0` guard; all `Decimal`.
- **(d)** Hand-traced replay: grind fires Slow (i=5–7) then Med (i=8,9) on depth while fast_drop < 0.08;
  crash (i=13) fires Fast on speed (0.0857) despite sub-Med peak-drawdown — Fast genuinely distinct.
- **(e)** `calibrate_threshold` quantile-rank × margin, clamped `[0,1]`, empty ⇒ 0 fail-safe,
  distribution-agnostic; profile serde round-trips.

Two non-blocking forward notes, no action for this SPIKE: (1) the `slow_dd < med_dd` invariant isn't
enforced in `CircuitBreaker::new` — can't arise on the calibrated path, a `debug_assert` would harden
hand-built QE-212 profiles; (2) the intentional asymmetry where a `0` threshold disables Fast but fires
Slow/Med (uncalibrated fail-safe) — a conscious choice.

No outstanding comments. Approved for merge.
