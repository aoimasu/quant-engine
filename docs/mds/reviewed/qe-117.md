## Decision summary

`WalkForward::windows` generates `Window{index,train,validate}` over `0..n_bars`: Rolling slides a
fixed-width train `[origin,te)`, Anchored grows `[0,te)`; `validate` starts a `purge+embargo` gap
(`purge=lookback+label_horizon`, QE-113) after `te`, so `Window::windows_disjoint` (same predicate as
`cv::Fold`) holds for every window (AC1). `run<A>(n_bars,&mut archive,on_window)` threads ONE caller-owned
archive through every transition without reset (AC2; QE-118 owns archive internals); the per-window
callback displaces degraded entries while retaining the rest. Embargo folded into the train‚Üívalidate gap
(conservative).

## Review (pass 1) ‚Äî Approved

Principal-level review of `d593539`. Both ACs genuinely met; window geometry correct in both modes with
no off-by-one; the leakage invariant holds for every window. No real bugs.

- **(a) Geometry, no off-by-one.** `train_end = origin+train`; Rolling `[origin,te)` slides by `step`,
  Anchored `[0,te)` grows; `validate=[te+gap, te+gap+validate)`, `gap=purge+embargo`. Stop is
  `validate_end > n_bars` after computing the range, so a validate ending exactly at `n_bars` is kept and
  no partial window emitted. Loop terminates; degenerate `train/validate==0` break before push.
- **(b) Leakage-free for every window.** `windows_disjoint` is the identical `|tr‚àíval| > L+H` predicate as
  `cv::Fold`; validate is always after train so the binding pair separation is `purge+embargo+1 ‚â• L+H+1 >
  span` ‚Äî strictly disjoint with margin. Zero-gap contrast genuinely leaks (bars 49/50), not a tautology.
- **(c) Archive carry.** `run<A,F>` threads one `&mut A` with no reset. AC2 test substantive: strategy 1
  (seeded pre-run, untouched) persists ‚áí no reset; strategy 2 carries 0.20‚Üí0.25; strategy 3 selectively
  displaced below floor; `len==2` confirms selective not wholesale.
- **(d) Edges.** `step.max(1)`, `train/validate==0` ‚áí no windows, partial-validate stop all handled/tested.
- **Embargo-in-gap.** Defensible ‚Äî strictly conservative for the within-window AC1 invariant.
