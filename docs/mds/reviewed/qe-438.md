## Review verdict — [Approved] (0 blocking, 2 non-blocking)
1. **Core invariant confirmed.** Scoring (`member_weights → cap_weights([1/k…], pool_capacities[members], AUM)`) and deploy (`cap_or_equal → cap_weights([1/k…], strategy_capacities(chromosomes), AUM)`) both derive from the **same** extracted `train.rs::strategy_capacities` at the **same** `TARGET_AUM_USD` and same per-strategy capacity model → the gross optimize-X-deploy-Y gap is closed.
2. **Non-vacuous.** Non-binding → both paths go through `weighted_combined(series,[1/k…])` (equal). Binding → concrete tests: `objective_scores_on_deployed_weights_when_caps_bind` (0.03 equal vs 0.003 diluted) and `deployed_weighting_can_flip_the_membership_ranking` (equal prefers {0,1}; capped flips to {1,2}).
3. **QE-430 composition intact.** `weighting` affects only mean + CVaR (via `combined_returns_weighted`); the correlation penalty stays on the **raw** member series (weight-independent), `corr_penalty_effective_n` unchanged.
4. **Firewall clean.** Intra-crate reuse of `crate::capacity`/`crate::stress`; no `qe_wfo` import in `crates/ensemble/src`; no `qe-ensemble→qe-wfo` edge; firewall passes.
5. **No golden moved** — the committed goldens invoke neither `search_portfolio` nor `run_train`, so ensemble-scoring changes can't reach them; determinism + backtest golden guards pass.

### Non-blocking findings (accepted)
1. **The "byte-identical" equal-weight claim is inaccurate — it is numerically equal (≤ 1 ULP), NOT byte-identical, for k ≥ 3.** The delegating originals now route through `weighted_combined = Σ rᵢ·(1/k)` vs the pre-QE-438 `(Σ rᵢ)/k` (empirically: `[0.02,0,0.01]` → old `0.01` vs new `0.009999999999999998`). **Zero material impact:** committed goldens don't invoke the ensemble search, determinism holds, and the "reduces to equal-weight" AC compares new-`CapacityCapped` to new-`EqualWeight` (byte-exact). The `deployed_combined_reuses_weighted_combined` equal-weight assertion is therefore a tautology. **Follow-up:** either correct the wording to "numerically equal (≤1 ULP)" or restore the old `(Σr)/k` body on the `EqualWeight` branch for strict byte-identity (recommended if a future golden ever routes through the search).
2. Two second-order scored-vs-sealed weight differences remain (gross gap still closed): the sealed weights get `hash_stable` rounding that scoring doesn't, and the QE-436 parsimony swap can change the deployed genome's capacity after scoring — both only among equal-fitness genomes.
