## Review verdict — [Approved]
STDOUT safety proven, not argued: CLI builds telemetry with `writer: OutputStream::Stderr` and `init()` routes Stderr arms to `std::io::stderr()` — no path reaches stdout; verified empirically (stdout = only `{"t":"progress"…}`/`{"t":"error"…}`, span+`info!` on stderr). No double-init: `init_cli_telemetry()` called once in `main`, non-fatal on failure; server inits once via `from_env()`. Health-quiet robust: TraceLayer on the inner `api` router, `path.ends_with("/health")` covers `/health` + `/api/health`, health at `debug_span!` vs `info_span!` elsewhere, response emitted at the span's level so an `info` filter drops only health spam. Firewall/deny: `qe-cli → qe-telemetry` legal (cli = composition root); Cargo.lock diff is edges-only (no new `[[package]]`), so deny stays green. Three per-command spans are the minimal correct way to satisfy the CLI-stage-spans AC.

Non-blocking nit (recorded, not actioned): `path.ends_with("/health")` would also quiet a hypothetical future `/api/<x>/health`; an exact `== "/health"` match would be tighter. No such route today.
